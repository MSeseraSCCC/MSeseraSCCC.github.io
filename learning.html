Hello!

To say hello in French, use "bonjour" for during the morning and the day, and use "bonsoir" during the evening.

Goodbye.

To say goodbye, use "au revoir" regardless of the time of day. This is pronounced "arr vwah"

Now on to Hello! My name is _______"

You already know how to say hello, now follow up with "je m'appelle __________" which sounds just like it looks. Simple, right?

Hello?

Speaking on the phone is a little bit different in French than in English. Instead of saying bonjour to answer the phone, you say "All√¥?" This essentially translates to "Hello?" as well as bonjour, but it is used specifically for phone calls.

Yes/No

To say yes, you want to use "oui" (pronounced "we"), and to san no, use "non" which sounds similar to a combination between "no" and a nasally "nah" in English.
